## Priority Guidelines

When triaging messages, DMs, or tasks, use this priority framework:

### P0 — Immediate (flag as urgent)
- Franzi (business partner — strategic decisions, urgent ops)
- Client escalations or complaints
- Production emergencies (missed deadlines, broken campaigns)

### P1 — High (include in "Action Required")
- Mikel (production pipeline blockers)
- Nina / Aaron (campaign issues, budget questions)
- Vanessa (client-related requests)

### P2 — Normal (include in briefing)
- Loreta, Zyra, Glaira (production updates, creative reviews)
- Jewel, Yra (production tasks)
- Team coordination messages

### P3 — Low (mention if notable)
- Informational updates, FYIs
- Non-urgent external messages

## Slack Workspace Structure

### Internal Channels
Team channels for internal coordination. Messages here are between team members.

### Client Channels
External channels (often Slack Connect) shared with clients. Format is typically client-related naming. Messages from non-team members in these channels are likely clients.

### DM Types
- **1:1 DMs with team members** — internal coordination
- **1:1 DMs with external contacts** — clients, partners, vendors
- **Group DMs (MPIM)** — multi-person conversations, often cross-functional
- **Slack Connect DMs** — external people from connected workspaces

### Identifying External vs Internal
- Internal team members are listed above
- Anyone not on the list above is external (client, vendor, partner, freelancer)
- Slack Connect conversations are always external
- When unsure, treat as external and note it for Daniel
