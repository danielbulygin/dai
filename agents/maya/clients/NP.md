# Ninepine — Creative Strategy Context

## Brand Overview

Premium European women's activewear and basics. Never-out-of-stock philosophy — no seasonal or temporary products. Low-SKU, high-concentration model: 80-90% of revenue from 10-20% of SKUs. Price point €95-€150.

**Sister brands:** NOSO (men's), Comis (self-tan subscription)

## Product Lines

| Line | Creative Focus |
|------|---------------|
| **Asana** | Core activewear pants — comfort, quality fabric, not see-through |
| **Denim** | 6 fits — comfort-first positioning vs traditional denim brands |
| **Tailored/Workwear** | Crepe pant, stretchy jersey — highest growth potential per founder |
| **Winter/Fleece** | Seasonal — fleece leggings, winter jeans. High margin, low returns |
| **Knitwear/Merino** | Developing — cashmere sweater as "halo product" |

## Target Customer

- **Median age:** 42 (online), 52 (physical store)
- **Best cohort:** Women 55-60 — lowest returns, highest disposable income
- **Mindset:** Not fashion people. Want comfort. Want to see the product would look good on THEM, not a model with a certain look
- **Education level:** Educated, intellectually curious — "a lot of smart people that want to relate to this polymath thing"

## Creative Direction

### What Works
- **Lo-fi, organic-feeling content** — No-Ads Ads (F16) perform best for TOF
- **Comfort-first messaging** — not fashion, not fitness. Comfort and quality
- **Customer language** — "never see-through", "so comfortable I wore them all week"
- **Localization via voiceover** — biggest growth lever. German VO on existing English ads
- **Body diversity** — real women, not fitness models
- **Product clarity at key moments** — show the fabric, the fit, the quality difference

### What Does NOT Work
- Fashion-forward positioning (they are NOT a fashion brand)
- Fitness/athletic messaging (not activewear in the gym sense)
- Polished studio shoots that feel too "ad-like"
- Social proof with huge numbers (product superiority is "proportional, not absolute" — 50% better, not 3x better)
- Price-focused hooks (€99-€150 requires quality justification, not discounting)
- TikTok Shop vibes (price point too high for impulse buys)

### Proven Coordinates
- **F16 × A01** (No-Ads Ad + Problem→Solution): Top TOF performer
- **F01 × A14** (Talking Head + Testimonial): Strong MOF, authentic reviews
- **F05 × A07** (VO + B-roll + Lifestyle): Premium feel, brand building
- **F01 × A01** (Talking Head + Problem→Solution): Reliable workhorse

### Underexplored Opportunities
- **F17 × A01** (Two-Person + Problem→Solution): Trending format, natural conversation
- **F03 × A09** (Product Demo + Science): Fabric technology, quality demonstration
- **F02 × A04** (Interview + Founder Story): Kousha's story is compelling

## Markets

- **Primary:** DACH, Nordics (Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland), Netherlands
- **Growth:** UK (down 40% YoY — Amazon competition), Germany (flat), US
- **Creative implication:** Multi-language creative is essential. Voiceover localization is the highest-leverage tactic.

## Key Quotes (Kousha)

- "It's not like our 99 Euro Asana is three times better than the 33 Euro Amazon version. It's 50% better."
- "We have a lot of educated customers... smart people that want to relate to this polymath thing"
- Customer mindset: "not looking to get inspired by a model with a certain look — they want to see that the product would look good on them"
