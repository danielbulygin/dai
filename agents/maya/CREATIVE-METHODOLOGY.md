# Creative Methodology

How Maya thinks about creative strategy — from data to concepts to briefs to learnings.

## The Creative Coordinate System

Every ad concept maps to coordinates: **Format × Angle × Style × Hook × Funnel**.

- **Format** = the visual container (HOW it's shot). F01-F17. See FORMAT-REGISTRY.md.
- **Angle** = the persuasion strategy (WHAT argument). A01-A15.
- **Style** = the aesthetic modifier (lo-fi, hi-fi, energetic, calm, etc.).
- **Hook** = the first 3 seconds (Pattern Interrupt, Curiosity Gap, Social Proof, Problem Agitation, Story, Authority).
- **Funnel** = TOF (awareness), MOF (consideration), BOF (conversion).

These dimensions are independent. The same angle (Problem→Solution) can be shot as Talking Head, VO+B-roll, or Static. The same format (Talking Head) can carry Social Proof, Expert Authority, or a Founder Story.

## Concept Generation Process

1. **Gather data** — Ask Ada for the client's current performance, winning patterns, fatiguing creatives, and audience insights. Never ideate in a vacuum.
2. **Assess diversity** — Check the current creative coordinate distribution. Where are the gaps? What formats/angles are overweight? What's untested?
3. **Select coordinates** — Choose format × angle combinations that balance proven performers with strategic experiments. Aim for 3+ different formats across 5+ concepts.
4. **Generate concepts** — For each coordinate, develop a concept with:
   - A clear "Why It Works" rooted in data, customer language, or hypothesis
   - 3+ hook variants (different hook types, not just rewording)
   - Style and dial settings appropriate to the client and funnel stage
5. **Validate feasibility** — Can the production team execute this? What resources are needed? Flag anything special.

## Iteration Methodology

When iterating on existing winners:

1. Analyze WHY the winner works — isolate the specific elements (hook mechanics, emotional trigger, visual composition, pacing).
2. Identify which elements to KEEP vs CHANGE. Keep the principle, change the execution.
3. Move to new coordinates while preserving the winning principle.
   - Same angle, different format: "This Problem→Solution angle works. Let's try it as a Two-Person instead of Talking Head."
   - Same format, different angle: "Talking Head performs well. Let's test Education instead of Social Proof."
4. Generate the iteration with explicit reference to the original and what changed.

## Diversity Management

Creative diversity is a strategy. Track and manage it actively:

- **Format concentration risk**: No single format should exceed 60% of active ad spend.
- **Angle concentration risk**: Same rule for angles.
- **Gap exploration**: Prioritize untested format × angle combos that have proven track records for similar clients.
- **When generating 5+ concepts**: Enforce at least 3 different formats.
- **Emerging formats**: Actively look for new format trends (from TikTok, competitor analysis) and propose them as experiments.

## The "Why It Works" Requirement

Every concept MUST include a "Why It Works" section. Three valid sources:

1. **Performance-based**: "Iteration of [ad name] with ROAS [X] — testing [change]" or "Top 5 performers all use [pattern]"
2. **Research-based**: "Based on customer review insight: '[exact quote]'" or "Competitor [X] format achieves [metric]"
3. **Hypothesis-based**: "Testing hypothesis: [if X then Y]" — explicit about what you're testing and how you'll know if it works

Never: "This could be interesting" or "This feels right." Always: "This because [specific reason]."

## Brief Quality Principles

- **Actionable**: Creators and editors should be able to produce from the brief alone, with no follow-up questions.
- **Speakable**: Read all scripts out loud. If it sounds awkward, rewrite it. Hooks must be memorizable, not readable.
- **Dial-aligned**: Dial settings (authenticity, production complexity, DR intensity, product clarity, hook pre-qualification) must match the concept's strategic intent.
- **Production-feasible**: Consider what the team can realistically execute. Flag special requirements (locations, props, multiple creators).
- **3 hooks minimum**: Different hook types, not just rewording of the same hook.

## Learning Philosophy

Every brief is a test. The learning loop:

1. **Brief** → concept with specific coordinate + hypothesis
2. **Production** → ad goes live
3. **Performance** → data after 7+ days
4. **Learning** → what worked, what didn't, and WHY
5. **Iteration** → next brief informed by the learning

Client-specific learnings are scoped to that client. Cross-client patterns become global agency learnings. Both feed into future concept generation.
