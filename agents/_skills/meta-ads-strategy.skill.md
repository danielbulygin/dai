# Meta Ads Strategy Skill

Comprehensive knowledge for creating and optimizing Meta/Facebook advertising strategies, including direct response principles, hook frameworks, creative dials, and testing methodology.

## Direct Response Principles

### The Core Formula

Every high-performing ad follows this structure:

1. **Hook** (0-3 sec) - Stop the scroll, create curiosity
2. **Problem/Agitation** - Identify pain point, make it feel urgent
3. **Solution** - Introduce product as the answer
4. **Proof** - Social proof, results, credibility
5. **CTA** - Clear, specific call to action

### Key DR Copywriting Rules

1. **Lead with benefits, not features**
   - BAD: "Contains 500mg of Vitamin C"
   - GOOD: "Wake up with energy that lasts all day"

2. **One idea per ad** - Pick ONE angle and commit. Do not try to say everything.

3. **Write like you talk** - Read it out loud. If it sounds weird, rewrite it. Avoid jargon unless your audience uses it.

4. **Specificity sells**
   - BAD: "Thousands of happy customers"
   - GOOD: "12,847 5-star reviews"

5. **Create urgency without being sleazy** - Use scarcity (limited stock), time-sensitivity (seasonal relevance), and social proof momentum ("selling fast").

## Hook Frameworks

Every script should have 3 hook variants for A/B testing. Hooks are the most critical part of any ad.

| Hook # | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Hook A | Primary hook (strongest concept) |
| Hook B | Alternative emotion/angle |
| Hook C | Alternative structure (e.g., question vs. statement) |

### 1. Pattern Interrupt

**Purpose:** Break the scroll by doing something unexpected.

| Type | Template | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Mid-Action Start | Start with action already happening | *Already pouring product* "...and this is why I can't go back" |
| Unexpected Juxtaposition | "[Unrelated thing] taught me about [category]" | "My dentist taught me about skincare" |
| Fourth Wall Break | Look directly at camera, pause, then speak | *Stares* "We need to talk about your morning routine" |
| Sound Interrupt | Unexpected sound or silence | *Record scratch* "Yeah, that's me..." |

### 2. Curiosity Gap

**Purpose:** Create an information gap viewers need to close.

| Type | Template | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Secret Reveal | "The [industry] secret they don't want you to know" | "The skincare secret dermatologists keep quiet" |
| Discovery | "I was today years old when I learned..." | "I was today years old when I learned you can [benefit]" |
| Unexpected Source | "A [unexpected person] taught me [thing]" | "A bartender taught me the best productivity hack" |
| Contrarian | "Why [common advice] is actually wrong" | "Why drinking 8 glasses of water is actually wrong" |

### 3. Social Proof

**Purpose:** Leverage herd mentality and FOMO.

| Type | Template | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Trend Alert | "Why is everyone suddenly [doing thing]?" | "Why is everyone suddenly switching to [product]?" |
| POV Format | "POV: You finally [action]" | "POV: You finally found a [product] that actually works" |
| Reaction | "[Person] reacts to [product/result]" | "My husband's reaction when he saw my skin" |
| Bandwagon | "[Number] people can't be wrong" | "2 million people switched this month" |

### 4. Problem Agitation

**Purpose:** Make the pain point feel urgent.

| Type | Template | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Direct Call-Out | "If you [symptom], this is for you" | "If you wake up tired every morning, this is for you" |
| Relatable Struggle | "Tell me you [problem] without telling me" | "Tell me you have dry skin without telling me" |
| Frustration | "I'm so tired of [common problem]" | "I'm so tired of products that promise but don't deliver" |
| Question | "Why does [frustrating thing] always happen?" | "Why does my energy crash at 3pm every single day?" |

### 5. Story Hooks

**Purpose:** Pull viewers into a narrative.

| Type | Template | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Transformation | "6 months ago I couldn't [thing], now..." | "6 months ago I couldn't look in the mirror, now..." |
| Mistake | "I made a huge mistake with [category]" | "I made a huge mistake with my skincare routine" |
| Journey Start | "This is day 1 of [experiment]" | "This is day 1 of only using [product]" |
| Before/After Setup | "What [time period] of [product] looks like" | "What 30 days of [product] actually looks like" |

### 6. Authority/Credibility

**Purpose:** Establish trust immediately.

| Type | Template | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Expert Intro | "As a [credential], I never expected..." | "As a nutritionist, I never expected to recommend this" |
| Years of Experience | "After [X] years of [thing], I finally found..." | "After 10 years of bad sleep, I finally found..." |
| Tested Everything | "I've tried every [category] and THIS is..." | "I've tried every protein powder and THIS is different" |
| Professional Secret | "[Profession] don't want you to know this" | "Makeup artists don't want you to know this trick" |

### Hook Writing Rules

- **3-Second Test:** Can you communicate the core idea in 3 seconds? If not, simplify.
- **Specificity Wins:** "This gave me 3 extra hours of energy every day" beats "This changed my life."
- **Native over Polished:** TOF hooks should feel like organic content. Overly produced = ad = skip.
- **Match Energy to Funnel:** TOF = high energy/curiosity, MOF = moderate/informative, BOF = calmer/offer-focused.

## Creative Dials System

Use these dials to calibrate creative direction. Confirm dial settings before generating any concepts.

### Authenticity (0-1)

Controls how "ad-like" vs. "organic/native" the content feels.

| Level | Feel | CTA Style | Brand Mentions | Benefits Style |
|-------|------|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| 0-0.3 | Clearly an ad | "Shop now! Link in bio!" | Multiple, prominent | Listed/stacked |
| 0.4-0.6 | Branded content | "Check it out" | Natural, 1-2x | Woven into story |
| 0.7-1.0 | Friend shared this | Implicit/none | May not name directly | Experienced, not stated |

At high authenticity (0.7+): Omit discount codes, omit explicit pricing, use discovery narrative, encourage natural delivery imperfections.

### Production Complexity (0-1)
- 0 = Simple UGC (phone footage, natural light, one-take)
- 1 = Cinematic (studio, pro lighting, multiple angles, graphics)

### DR-Intensity (0-1)
- 0 = Brand-focused (awareness, no hard sell)
- 1 = Aggressive DR (urgency, scarcity, strong CTA)

### Product Clarity (0-1)
- 0 = Lifestyle-first (product subtle/background)
- 1 = Product hero (clear shots, features visible)

### Hook Pre-qualification (0-1)

Controls how much the hook itself filters/targets the audience.

| Level | Feel | Best For |
|-------|------|----------|
| 0-0.3 | Universal hook - anyone could stop | TOF awareness, volume plays |
| 0.4-0.6 | Situation-based - hints at context | MOF consideration |
| 0.7-1.0 | Audience-specific - self-selecting | BOF conversion, broad targeting |

### Funnel Stage (TOF/MOF/BOF)

| Stage | Focus | Pacing | Hook Energy |
|-------|-------|--------|-------------|
| TOF | Awareness, entertainment-first | Fast cuts, high energy | Curiosity, pattern interrupt |
| MOF | Consideration, address objections | Medium pace, more detail | Informative, relatable |
| BOF | Conversion, offer-focused | Can be slower, focus on CTA | Offer-led, urgency |

## Script Structure Formats

### Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS)
1. Hook: Identify the problem
2. Agitate: Make it feel urgent, relatable
3. Solve: Introduce product as the answer
4. Proof: Social proof, results
5. CTA: Clear next step

### Testimonial/UGC Format
1. Hook: Personal discovery moment
2. Background: "I used to struggle with..."
3. Discovery: "Then I found [product]..."
4. Experience: Specific results and benefits
5. Recommendation: Authentic endorsement + soft CTA

### Demo/Tutorial Format
1. Hook: Promise a result
2. Setup: Quick context
3. Demo: Show product in use, step by step
4. Result: Show the outcome
5. CTA: Where to get it

## Creative Testing Methodology

### Test Hierarchy
1. **Concept/Angle** (biggest lever) - Test completely different approaches
2. **Hook** (second biggest) - Same body, different hooks (always test 3)
3. **Body/Format** - Same hook, different storytelling
4. **CTA** - Same everything, different calls to action

### Testing Rules
- Test one variable at a time when iterating
- When launching new concepts, overproduce (3x what you need) and let data pick winners
- Kill underperformers fast, scale winners gradually
- Hook rate benchmarks: 30%+ = excellent, 25-30% = solid, 20-25% = needs work, below 20% = kill or rework

## Localization: Transcreation

When creating ads in multiple languages, use transcreation (creative translation), not literal translation.

**The goal: Same emotion, same effectiveness, different words.**

- Native speakers should not be able to tell it was originally written in another language
- Use idioms native to the target language
- Follow natural syntax of the target language
- Use phrases people actually say, not textbook language
