# Meta Ads Compliance Skill

Comprehensive knowledge for checking ad creative against Meta/Facebook advertising policies. Use this to validate ads before launch and diagnose rejection reasons.

## Prohibited Content

### Health Claims
- No "miracle cure" language
- No guarantees of specific health outcomes
- No before/after images that imply unrealistic results
- ALLOWED: "may help," "supports," "designed to"

### Personal Attributes Policy

Meta prohibits ads that assert or imply personal attributes about the viewer. This is one of the most common rejection reasons.

**Prohibited (directly addressing the viewer's attributes):**
- "Are you overweight?"
- "Struggling with debt?"
- "Do you have acne?"
- "Are you lonely?"

**Allowed (framing as general statements):**
- "Many people find it hard to manage weight"
- "A solution for those looking to improve their finances"
- "Skincare that works for problem skin"

**Key principle:** Frame messaging as general benefits or experiences, never as direct assertions about the individual viewer's condition, identity, or situation.

### Weight Loss Restrictions
- No before/after body images
- No specific weight loss claims ("lose 10 lbs in 2 weeks")
- No images of measuring tapes around waists
- ALLOWED: Focus on energy, feeling good, lifestyle improvements, wellness

### Financial Claims
- No income guarantees ("make $10K/month")
- No "get rich quick" implications
- No unrealistic earnings claims
- Required: Appropriate risk disclaimers for any financial product

### Misleading Content
- No deceptive offers or clickbait
- No fake news formatting
- No fake "close" buttons or UI elements
- Landing page must match ad claims

## Required Disclaimers

### Supplements
Must include:
- "These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA"
- "This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease"

### Testimonials
Must include:
- "Results may vary"
- "Individual results not guaranteed"

### Financial Products
- Appropriate risk disclaimers required
- Must comply with local financial advertising regulations

## Creative Restrictions

### Text in Images
- No more than 20% text in thumbnail/preview images (soft rule but affects delivery significantly)
- Keep text overlays concise - max 5 words per screen
- Test on mobile to ensure readability

### Shocking/Sensational Content
- No excessive violence or graphic content
- No sensationalized imagery designed to shock
- No graphic surgical or medical imagery
- No exaggerated or misleading claims designed to provoke fear

### Audio
- Ads should make sense without sound (many users browse muted)
- Subtitles required for all talking-head content
- Music should support, not compete with voice audio

### Before/After Imagery
- No before/after body transformation images for weight loss
- Before/after for skincare, hair, etc. must show realistic results
- Avoid implying that results are guaranteed or typical
- If using before/after, include "results may vary" disclaimer

## Targeting Restrictions

These affect who can see ads - ensure campaigns respect these:

| Category | Restriction |
|----------|-------------|
| Alcohol | 21+ only (US), varies by country |
| Dating | 18+ |
| Financial services | Various restrictions by country |
| Health/medical | Additional review may be required |
| Political/social | Disclaimer and authorization required |

## Landing Page Requirements

- Landing page content must match ad claims
- Must have a clear, accessible privacy policy
- All links must be functional
- No auto-play video with sound
- Page must be fully functional (no broken elements)
- No pop-ups that prevent viewing content
- No deceptive download buttons

## Common Rejection Reasons and Fixes

### 1. Personal Attributes Violation
**Symptom:** Ad rejected for "personal attributes"
**Common cause:** Copy directly addresses viewer's condition
**Fix:** Reframe from "you" statements to general benefits
- BEFORE: "Tired of your acne?"
- AFTER: "Finally, skincare that works on problem skin"

### 2. Misleading Claims
**Symptom:** Ad rejected for "misleading claims"
**Common cause:** Overpromising results
**Fix:** Soften language, add disclaimers
- BEFORE: "Guaranteed to clear your skin in 7 days"
- AFTER: "Designed to help improve skin clarity. Results may vary."

### 3. Before/After Policy
**Symptom:** Ad rejected for "before and after" content
**Common cause:** Showing body transformation images
**Fix:** Focus on the experience or product itself, not body changes
- Remove side-by-side body comparisons
- Show product usage and lifestyle instead

### 4. Health Claims
**Symptom:** Ad rejected for "health claims"
**Common cause:** Making medical-sounding claims
**Fix:** Use softer language and proper disclaimers
- BEFORE: "Cures insomnia"
- AFTER: "Supports better sleep quality*" (*Results may vary)

### 5. Low-Quality or Sensational Content
**Symptom:** Ad rejected for "low quality" or "sensational"
**Common cause:** Clickbait hooks, shocking imagery, exaggerated claims
**Fix:** Tone down language, use authentic imagery
- BEFORE: "DOCTORS HATE THIS ONE TRICK"
- AFTER: "A simple approach that many are finding helpful"

### 6. Text-Heavy Image
**Symptom:** Ad has very low delivery/reach
**Common cause:** Too much text in the image or thumbnail
**Fix:** Reduce text to under 20% of image area, use overlay text sparingly

### 7. Non-Functional Landing Page
**Symptom:** Ad rejected for "non-functional landing page"
**Common cause:** Broken links, missing privacy policy, pop-up interference
**Fix:** Test all links, ensure privacy policy is accessible, remove aggressive pop-ups

## Compliance Review Checklist

When reviewing an ad for compliance, check each item:

### Copy Check
- [ ] No personal attributes assertions (not addressing viewer's condition directly)
- [ ] No unrealistic or guaranteed outcome claims
- [ ] No "miracle," "cure," "guaranteed" language for health products
- [ ] No specific weight loss claims
- [ ] No income guarantees for financial products
- [ ] Appropriate disclaimers included (supplements, testimonials, financial)
- [ ] No misleading urgency (fake countdown timers, false scarcity)

### Visual Check
- [ ] No before/after body images for weight loss
- [ ] No shocking, graphic, or violent imagery
- [ ] Text covers less than 20% of image area
- [ ] No fake UI elements (close buttons, notifications)
- [ ] Product shown accurately (not misleading packaging or size)

### Targeting Check
- [ ] Age restrictions set correctly for product category
- [ ] Geographic restrictions respected
- [ ] Special ad category selected if applicable (housing, employment, credit, politics)

### Landing Page Check
- [ ] URL is functional and loads correctly
- [ ] Content matches ad claims
- [ ] Privacy policy is present and accessible
- [ ] No auto-play audio
- [ ] No aggressive pop-ups blocking content

### Platform-Specific
- [ ] Ad complies with Meta's current Advertising Standards
- [ ] Special ad category declared if required
- [ ] Content suitable for all placements selected (feed, stories, reels, audience network)

## Tips for Avoiding Rejections

1. **When in doubt, soften the language.** It is easier to make compliant copy compelling than to get non-compliant copy approved.
2. **Use "may," "designed to," "supports" instead of definitive claims.**
3. **Frame benefits as experiences** ("I felt more energized") rather than guarantees ("You WILL have more energy").
4. **Test with the worst-case interpretation** - if a Meta reviewer reading your ad uncharitably could find a violation, rewrite it.
5. **Keep a rejection log** - track what gets rejected and why to avoid repeat issues.
6. **Check Meta's current policies** before each campaign - policies change frequently.
